Core Formulas
💧
Solid Stream Flow
GPM from a solid bore nozzle
GPM = 29.7 × D² × √NP
D = nozzle diameter (inches)  |  NP = nozzle pressure (psi)
Handline NP = 50 psi  |  Master Stream NP = 80 psi
√50 ≈ 7.07  |  √80 ≈ 8.94  |  √100 = 10  |  √36 = 6  |  √25 = 5
↩️
Nozzle Reaction
Force pushing back on the firefighter
NR = 1.57 × d² × NP
Solid stream nozzles — d = nozzle diameter, NP = nozzle pressure
NR = 0.0505 × Q × √NP
Fog nozzles — Q = GPM flowing, NP = nozzle pressure (100 psi for fog)
🔥
Friction Loss
Theoretical formula
FL = C × Q² × L
C = hose coefficient (see table)  |  Q = GPM ÷ 100  |  L = hose length ÷ 100
Example: 200' of 3" hose @ 300 GPM → C=0.8, Q=3, L=2 → 0.8×9×2 = 14 psi
🚒
Pump Discharge Pressure
Total pressure the pump must produce
PDP = NP + FL + AL + EP
NP = nozzle pressure  |  FL = friction loss  |  AL = appliance loss  |  EP = elevation pressure
EP = 0.5 × H (feet)
H = height in feet above pump  |  In buildings: 5 psi × (floors − 1)
Net PDP = PDP − Intake Pressure
Used when being supplied by another pumper
🏊
Pool Capacity
Gallons in a swimming pool
C = 7.5 × L × W × D
Rectangular — L=length, W=width, D=avg depth (all in feet)
C = 7.5 × π × r² × D
Round — π=3.14, r=radius (diameter÷2), D=avg depth
7.5 = gallons per cubic foot of water
💦

Inspection Procedures
Walk-around & pretrip rules
Start: driver's door, clockwise
Begin walk-around at driver's door; proceed clockwise around apparatus
Always chock wheels when parked
Required every time the driver/operator exits the cab
Steering play: ≤ 10° either direction
Excessive play can indicate worn steering components
Air brake min pressure: 80 psi
Air pressure protection valve prevents air horns below 80 psi (560 kPa)
Never rely only on gauges
Always physically inspect all fluid levels — don't trust warning lights alone
Talk to the last driver for the most accurate account of how the vehicle operated.

Driver/Operator Legal & Safety
Regulations, liability & safe operations
Most accidents: intersections
Highest risk point during emergency response
Most costly accidents: backing
Backing accidents = significant % of all damage repair costs
License authority: Federal DOT
States/provinces may alter federal requirements as needed
Lights/siren: may exempt speed limits
Do NOT exempt from: yielding to other emergency vehicles, safe driving
Both driver AND department liable
Both can be held responsible in civil or criminal cases
All riders must wear seat belts before apparatus is put in motion. Gear is donned before boarding in most departments.

Tires, Brakes & GVWR
Key specs and replacement rules
Tire replacement: every 10 years
Per NFPA® 1911 — regardless of tread condition
All tires: same size & weight rating
Must match manufacturer specifications; based on gross axle weight ratings
Brake test: annually (NFPA® 1911)
Thorough testing required each year
GVWR = max safe operating weight
Maximum weight at which a vehicle can be safely operated on roadways in ideal conditions
ABS = straight-line braking
Antilock brakes keep apparatus tracking straight during emergency/heavy braking
